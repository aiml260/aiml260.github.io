AIML 260: Non-Euclidean Metrics and Lie Theory in Robotic Control

Prof. Weiqing Gu
Winter 2020/21

Course Description

Geometrical ways of describing configuration spaces of robotsâ€™ kinematics and dynamics are backbones in in modern robotics. Robotic control is the system that contributes to the movement of Robots. Bringing geometric methods to robot learning, optimization and control has lead to state of the art results in the field. Why do we need a non-Euclidean metric? Robots often operate in non-Euclidean (e.g. curved) spaces, and we need a way to describe distance on the curved space in order to do planning. In this course, we will first introduce basic control methods in a Euclidean space with Euclidean metrics. Then we will extend the control law to a non-Euclidean setting using non-Euclidean metrics. What is Lie theory and why is it useful? Lie theory is an area of mathematics that is largely used in modern robotics modeling and control. In this course, we will illustrate the use of Lie theory to model the configuration spaces and control nonlinear robotic systems. The effectiveness of the nonlinear controller will be illustrated with simulation examples.
